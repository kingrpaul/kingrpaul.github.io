It Was Okay

It was okay. It was not terrible, but it certainly was not great. The miniseries was probably better.

Some of the casting was poor, but Isaac and Ferguson did well. It's usually a shame for a movie maker to exchange an author's vision for their neighbor's expectations & sensibilities This keeps happening.

It doesn't help when he or she tells the audience how to interpret the story's ending just after the opening credits have finished. A well-told story leaves the viewer the pleasure of figuring that our for him or herself.

Having read the book tends to ruin a mediocre movie, even if it was only the first volume and a long time ago.

This is the second reboot of Frank Herbert's novel. Maybe it would be better to stick to rebooting botched things, things you're sure you can improve on.
